FOAM
CAFS
Advantage of using CAFS is the unique ability to produce a wide range
of foam qualities or foam types.
• Class A and B detergents can be used.
• Developed by US Navy and designed for class B fires.
• Credited for lightness, rapid cooling properties and range of use.
• New transport system for water “from a bucket now to a bubble”

---

Synthetic Foams
• Synthetic foams are based on synthetic surfactants. They
provide better flow and faster knockdown of flames, but
> ⚠️ **SAFETY — Action > Condition > Standard**
> limited post-fire security.
• Aqueous film forming foams (AFFF) are water-based and
frequently contain hydrocarbon-based surfactants. They have
the ability to spread over the surface of hydrocarbon-based
liquids.
• Alcohol-resistant aqueous film-forming foams (AR-AFFF) are
foams resistant to the action of alcohols and are able to form
a protective film when they are present.
PROTEIN FOAM
• Protein foams contain natural proteins as the foaming agents.
Unlike synthetic foams, protein foams are bio-degradable.
• They flow and spread slower, but provide a foam blanket that
is more heat-resistant and more durable.
• Protein foams include regular protein foam (P), fluoroprotein
foam (FP), film-forming fluoroprotein (FFFP), alcohol-resistant
fluoroprotein foam (AR-FP), and alcohol-resistant film-forming
fluoroprotein (AR-FFFP).
• Protein foam from non-animal sources is preferred because of
the possible threat of biological contaminants.

DEFINITION & APPLICATIONS
CAFS stands for Compressed Air Foam System. Foam aspiration
is enhanced by pressurized air within the pipe- arrangement
inside the vehicle, then carried through the hoselines to the
nozzle.
Class A Foam is used on solid combustible materials with an
application rate between 0.1% and 1.0%. Not in use in DFB
Class B Foam is used on Flammable liquids , Hydro Carbons &
Polar Solvent fires with an application Rate of 3% OR 6%.
HOW DOES CAFS
> ⚠️ **SAFETY — Action > Condition > Standard**
> PUT THE FIRE OUT?
> ⚠️ **SAFETY — Action > Condition > Standard**
> It is proven that CAFS attacks all three sides of the fire triangle
simultaneously. The foam blankets the fuel, thereby reducing
the fuel’s capacity to seek out a source of oxygen. The CAFS
solution adheres to ceilings and walls, more readily aiding in
rapid reduction in heat. Also, the opaque surfaces of the foam,
as it adheres to walls and ceilings, shield the fuel source from
radiant heat.”
(Brooks, 2005; Brooks et al, 2006).

Tunnel Details
DPT General Information
Dublin Tunnel has 2 bores, Northbound and Southbound, which are 4.5km long and are part
of the M50.
Nobody should enter a tunnel unless ordered by the O.I.C, typically D.O. Delta.
DN14A5 should only enter the unaffected bore, and only for incidents or exercises.
Dublin Tunnel has 19 Passenger Crossover Points (PCP’s), labelled 1 (Northern end) to 19
(Southern end).
> ⚠️ **SAFETY — Action > Condition > Standard**
> Never open a Vehicle Crossover Point.
The distances between the PCP’s vary but are, on average, 222m apart.
Approach incident on outer wall of unaffected bore – Beware Pedestrians
Position TRV past incident PCP.

Cooling Loop
• A cooling loop has been installed to allow water to
circulate from the pump back to the 1800ltr water tank
maintaining a cooler temperature within the pump
casing when pumping at moderate levels.
• This valve should be opened when no water is flowing
or intermittent water flow rates preventing the
activation of the Thermal Relief Valve.
• Whilst pumping at higher RPM generating larger
amounts of heat it is recommended to disperse this
heat via a delivery valve back to source.

---

Cooling Loop
•
As DN14A1 is a CAFS Pump, no foam solution will be
circulating within the pump casing and it is safe to open
while CAFS foam is being generated.
• Pump operators needs to be aware if generating foam
solution on a standard P2 4010 with a RTPP and cooling
loop installed , foam solution can circulate back into the
1800ltr tank contaminating the water with foam via the
cooling Loop
• Thermal protection for the Air compressor requires water to be flowing
> ⚠️ **SAFETY — Action > Condition > Standard**
> through deliveries to ensure overheating does not occur.
• This water absorbs the heat generated in the compressors oil system via a
heat exchanger
• Temperature must be maintained below 105 Degrees Celsius.
• Any Temperature above 110 Degrees Celsius will automatically cause the
compressor to disengage
• CAFS Appliances have cooling circuits installed to assist with this process.
> ⚠️ **SAFETY — Action > Condition > Standard**
> • Should a compressor temperature warning be displayed whilst pumping
at an incident the pump operator should consider releasing some water
via the Low-pressure deliveries to dissipate the heat generated.

Delivering CAFS
(after delivering water/foam solution)
(without clutch being disengaged)
1) Reduce throttle to idle.
2) Wait approx. 20 seconds (until Air Pressure has drained).
3) Select wet CAFS 3% or 6%  button on display(green icon)..hold for 1-2 secs.
4) Confirm foam pump is on and foam icon is illuminated (red border around)
if not manually press the button on.
5)   Confirm the AIR icon is on and illuminated (red border around)
if not manually press the button on.
6) Bring pump pressure to 7 bar.
7) Call "CAFS ON " and SLOWLY open CAFS delivery valve.
N.B. BRANCH OPERATOR'S SHOULD ONLY OPEN THE CAFS BRANCH WHEN THE HOSE LINE BEHIND THEM IS
FULLY CHARGED.
8) On completion of pumping, the entire system needs to be flushed of foam and of stored
pressure.
1.Set throttle to idle
2.Turn foam pump off by pressing the button beside the FOAM icon (top left of display) this should also turn off
the red border around the AIR icon, if the AIR border hasn’t turned off, press the button beside it (top right) to
turn it off.
3.Press the button beside the Flush icon.
> ⚠️ **SAFETY — Action > Condition > Standard**
> 4.Increase pump pressure to 3-4 bar, ensure the CAFS delivery valves is open and flush system until water runs
clear from the branch, flush both deliveries whether they were both used or not (for unused delivery, connect
second Storz length and allow to flush at low pressure until clear of foam and stored pressure)
5.Idle throttle
6.Disengage PTO and close CAFS deliveries
7.Replenish foam tank
> ⚠️ **SAFETY — Action > Condition > Standard**
> 8.Always beware of stored pneumatic pressure in the system until the entire system has been fully flushed and
stored pressure purged from it.
> ⚠️ **SAFETY — Action > Condition > Standard**
> 9.Always ensure DFB foam policies are adhered to.
